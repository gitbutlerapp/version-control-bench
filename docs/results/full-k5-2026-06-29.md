## Interpretation

`but+skill` remains the practical winner. It reduces mean wall time by 61.0% for Codex and 62.5% for Claude versus plain `git`, and it cuts task-relevant version-control commands by 82.9% and 84.4%.

The correctness caveat is narrower than the speed win but still real: Claude `but+skill` failed 2/5 split-commit runs with `GRAPH_WRONG`. Those runs selected the right content but rebuilt the commit stack in the wrong order.

`jj+skill` got a real chance and did not make the case. Codex `jj+skill` was clean at 25/25, but slower than `git` overall and slower than `but+skill` in every scenario. Claude `jj+skill` was both slower and less correct, with split-commit at 0/5.

## JJ Fairness Notes

The JJ arm was not handicapped by missing setup:

- `jj` was installed and measured as `jj 0.42.0`.
- Each fixture was prepared with `jj git init --colocate` before timing.
- The external skill `onevcat/skills@onevcat-jj` was installed into both `.codex/skills` and `.claude/skills` before timing.
- Raw Git writes and GitButler were blocked in the JJ arm.
- Setup, skill fetch/install, fixture creation, and dirty-state application were excluded from agent duration and command metrics.

The skill was selected as the top direct `jj` result from `npx skills find jj` on 2026-06-29. The raw JJ report records its source URL and file hash.
